# Mysore Travel Knowledge

## Routing

**Standard routing: Bangalore → Mysore**

Mysore is most commonly a 2–3 day extension from Bangalore, or a pivot point for Coorg and Ooty circuits. The NH275 Expressway (opened 2023) cut drive time significantly; Shatabdi Express (2 hours) remains the most reliable option for families and elderly.

Driving times:
- Bangalore → Mysore: 3–3.5 hours via NH275 Expressway (145 km)
- Coorg (Madikeri) → Mysore: 2.5–3 hours via NH275/SH17 (120 km)
- Hassan → Mysore: 2.5 hours (120 km; natural stop on Belur–Halebidu–Mysore circuit)
- Ooty → Mysore: 4 hours via Gudalur (170 km)

**Alternative routing:** Bangalore–Mysore Shatabdi Express (daily, 2 hours, ₹600–900 chair car) — preferred for families with toddlers and elderly. Mysore railway station is 2 km from the palace, auto-rickshaw distance.

## Mysore Palace & City Centre

**Key places:** Mysore Palace (Amba Vilas), Devaraja Market, St. Philomena's Church, Karanji Lake Nature Park

**Mysore Palace:**
- Entry: ₹100/Indians, ₹200/foreigners; audio guide ₹100; Sunday/holiday illumination (7–7:45 PM) ₹50 extra
- Wide marble corridors — strollers usable in outer sections but must be deposited at security for the Durbar Hall; carry toddlers inside. Elderly: mostly flat with low steps; limited free wheelchair rental at entrance. Shoes removed at entry — use the free token counter.
- Best time: 10–11 AM weekdays; avoid Sundays (1–2 hour queues). Sunday evening illumination spectacular but very crowded.
- Dasara (October, 10 days): 100,000 bulbs nightly — not suitable for toddlers or elderly in the open procession zone

**Karanji Lake Nature Park:**
- Entry: ₹30/adults, ₹15/children; butterfly enclosure ₹20 extra
- Flat paved paths throughout — fully accessible for elderly and strollers. Butterfly enclosure is reliably good for children aged 3–10.
- Best time: 8–10 AM for birds; butterfly enclosure most active 10 AM–noon

**Trust issue — silk and sandalwood diversions by autos:**
- Auto drivers divert tourists to private "government emporium" silk saree and sandalwood shops on commission. The framing is always "just passing nearby" or "free to look."
- Recommended approach: Genuine Mysore silk is sold only at the KSIC showroom (Mananthody Road, near the Palace). Authentic sandalwood products: KS&DL retail outlet near Lalitha Mahal Road. Tell your auto driver the destination only — no stops.

**City Centre food:**
- Local dishes: Mysore Masala Dosa (red chutney spread inside — distinct from Bangalore version, ₹80–120), Mysore Pak (dense ghee-and-gram-flour sweet; best at Guru Sweet Mart, Sayyaji Rao Road), Obbattu/Holige (jaggery-stuffed sweet flatbread)
- For toddlers and young kids: Plain idli with mild sambar at all Udupi restaurants; Mysore Pak in small pieces is fine for children 2+
- Vegetarian availability: Excellent — Brahmin vegetarian food culture dominates across all price points

## Chamundi Hills & Outer Mysore

**Key places:** Sri Chamundeshwari Temple (Chamundi Hills), Brindavan Gardens (KRS Dam, 19 km), Srirangapatna (16 km)

**Chamundi Hills Temple:**
- Entry: Free; special darshan ₹50 (30-min queue vs 1–2 hours general)
- Drive up 13 km road — shared autos ₹60/seat, private cab ₹300–400 return. The 1,008-step alternative is not suitable for elderly or toddlers. Hilltop is flat and paved.
- Best time: 7–9 AM; avoid Fridays and festival days (multi-hour queues)

**Brindavan Gardens:**
- Entry: ₹30/person; musical fountain show ₹90 (7 PM, 8 PM, 9 PM slots)
- Terraced gardens — lower terraces flat and accessible for strollers and elderly; upper sections have steps. Musical fountain good for young children but show crowds are dense; families with toddlers should sit at the outer edge.
- Best time: 4–6 PM for gardens; arrive 30 min early for fountain seating. Closed Monday mornings.

**Trust issue — Chamundi Hills and Brindavan "package" overcharging:**
- Station and palace-area touts quote ₹800–1,500 for a "Chamundi + Brindavan package." City buses cover both routes cheaply from the KSRTC bus stand: Bus No. 201 to Chamundi Hills (₹25/person), direct bus to Brindavan Gardens (₹35/person). Legitimate private cab to Brindavan is ₹400–500 return.
- Recommended approach: Use KSRTC buses for both. If hiring a cab, book via Ola at the bus stand — ₹100–150 cheaper than station touts for the same vehicle.

**Chamundi / Brindavan food:**
- Chamundi hilltop stalls: vada, bajji, coffee (₹30–60) — limited and overpriced
- Brindavan Gardens: KSTDC canteen serves standard South Indian meals (₹100–150/plate); acceptable quality
- For toddlers: Carry food from Mysore city; neither location has toddler-appropriate options
- Vegetarian availability: Entirely vegetarian at both locations

## General Mysore Notes

**Best time to visit:**
- October–March: Best overall. Dasara (October) is spectacular — 10-day festival, book accommodation 2–3 weeks ahead. November–March cool and dry (15–28°C), ideal for families and elderly.
- April–June: Warm (32–38°C) but manageable; palace and indoor sites are comfortable. Less harsh than North India summers.
- July–September: Moderate monsoon. Most attractions stay open; Brindavan is lush. Coorg roads get difficult in heavy spells.

**Transport pricing:**
- Ola and Rapido both operate in Mysore — reliable and recommended over negotiating autos for trips beyond 3 km.
- Auto-rickshaws: ₹50–120 within city; most drivers use meters — insist on meter use.
- Railway station to Mysore Palace: ₹80–100 by auto (2 km)

**Dietary notes:**
- Coconut features in most chutneys, gravies, and chutneys — relevant for coconut-allergic travellers. Tamarind and mustard oil are also base flavours throughout.
- Vegetarian options: Among the best quality and variety in South India at all price points.

**Medical:** JSS Hospital (private, Bannimantap) and Columbia Asia Hospital (Hebbal Ring Road) are the best private facilities. Government option: K.R. Hospital, adjacent to the palace. Mysore has stronger medical infrastructure than most Tier-2 tourist cities — quality care is accessible without transfer to Bangalore for most emergencies.
