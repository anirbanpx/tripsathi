# Puri Travel Knowledge

## Puri Overview

Coastal temple city in Odisha, on the Bay of Bengal. Major Hindu pilgrimage site (Jagannath Temple) and popular beach destination. 60 km from Bhubaneswar (nearest airport).

**Getting there:** Bhubaneswar airport → Puri: 1.5 hrs by cab (₹800–1,200). Puri railway station has direct trains from Kolkata, Delhi, Chennai.

**Key attractions:** Jagannath Temple, Puri Beach, Chilika Lake, Konark Sun Temple (65 km).

## Jagannath Temple — Critical Information for Non-Hindus

**Entry restriction:** The main Jagannath Temple complex **strictly prohibits entry to non-Hindus.** Guards check at all four gates (Singhadwara, Ashwadwara, Vyaghrhadwara, Hastidwara). Foreigners and non-Hindus are turned away regardless of dress or claims.

**For non-Hindu visitors and foreigners who want to see the temple:**
- **Raghunandan Library (Emar Math):** Building directly opposite the main gate (Singhadwara). Rooftop terrace gives a clear elevated view of the temple's 65-meter shikara (tower) and outer walls. Free entry. Open 7 AM–1 PM, 4 PM–8 PM. Ask for "terrace view" at the reception.
- **Nilachala Terrace:** Some adjacent buildings offer rooftop views; ask your hotel for current options.

**For Hindu visitors:**
- Free entry to the main temple.
- VIP darshan (faster queue): ₹100–200 via Shri Jagannath Temple Administration (SJTA) online booking.
- Dress code: Traditional attire preferred. Leather items (belts, bags) must be left outside.
- Queue times: 1–3 hours on normal days, 4–6+ hours on Ekadashi and festival days.
- Mobile phones and cameras not allowed inside.

**Rath Yatra (Chariot Festival):** Held annually in June–July. Millions of pilgrims; accommodation scarce and prices 5–10x normal. Advance booking 6+ months essential if visiting during this period.

## Chilika Lake

**Overview:** Asia's largest coastal lagoon, 1,100 sq km. Famous for Irrawaddy dolphins, migratory birds (Oct–Feb), and boat rides.

**Distance from Puri:** 45 km (Satapada entry point). Drive: 1 hour.

**Cab pricing — inflation warning:**
- Puri auto-rickshaws and taxis at the beach road frequently quote ₹3,000–5,000 for Puri → Satapada → Puri round trip.
- Fair market rate: ₹1,500–2,000 for the same trip in an AC sedan.
- **Recommended approach:** Book through your hotel front desk or a trusted operator. Hotel-arranged cabs are usually ₹1,800–2,200 with a reliable driver.
- Avoid flagging down vehicles near the beach or Jagannath Temple area for intercity trips.

**At Satapada:**
- Motorboat rides to dolphin-spotting point: ₹150–200 per person (government boat), ₹800–1,200 for private boat. Government boats are slower but significantly cheaper.
- Dolphin sightings: Nov–Feb is best. Off-season (May–Sep) sightings reduced.
- Duration: 2–3 hours including boat ride.

**With young kids:** Boat ride is generally calm. Life jackets provided. Suitable for kids 3+.

## Puri Beach

**Beach character:** Long (7 km), golden sand. Popular but can be crowded on weekends.

**Sea safety — critical:**
- Puri beach has strong undertow and unpredictable currents.
- **Red flag = no entry into water.** This is enforced by lifeguards. Do not enter the sea against flag warnings.
- Lifeguards are present **7 AM to 5 PM only.** No swimming after 5 PM.
- Children should be kept at the water's edge, never in beyond knee-depth, even during green flag days.
- The northern section of the beach (near fishing village) has fewer crowds but no lifeguards.

**Beach vendors:** Persistent souvenir and food vendors. Be firm with "no thank you" — they will move on.

## Child Food Situation in Puri — Important for Families

**Local cuisine is predominantly seafood-based:**
- Odisha coastal food features fish, crab, prawn cooked in mustard and spices.
- Most beach shacks and local restaurants have limited options for young children or those who don't eat seafood.
- Vegetarian options available in restaurants but often limited to dal, rice, aloo sabzi.

**Recommended approach for families with young children:**
- **Bring packaged foods from home or Bhubaneswar:** Biscuits (Marie, Parle-G), bread, instant noodles, peanut butter, cereal. Stock up before arriving in Puri.
- **Hotel restaurants** (mid-range and above) reliably offer continental items: toast, eggs, pasta, plain rice — suitable for fussy eaters.
- **South Indian restaurant near Puri bus stand:** Good idli, dosa, upma for kids. One or two options available.
- **Avoid beach shacks for young children's main meals** — hygiene standards vary and spice levels are high.

**Baby food / formula:** Available at chemists near the main market (Swargadwar area). Limited variety; bring preferred brands from home.

## Konark Sun Temple

**Distance:** 65 km from Puri, 1.5 hrs drive.
**Entry:** ₹600 for foreigners, ₹40 for Indian citizens. Open sunrise to sunset.
**UNESCO World Heritage Site.** 13th-century temple with explicit erotic carvings (discuss appropriateness if visiting with children).
**No entry inside the main sanctum** (structurally protected since 1903); exterior exploration only.

## Bhubaneswar Day Trip

60 km from Puri. Ancient temple city with 400+ temples (Lingaraj Temple being the largest). Worth half-day if time permits.

## General Puri Notes

**Best time:** October–February (cool, 18–28°C, calm seas). March–May: hot and humid. June–September: monsoon (rough seas, Rath Yatra in July).

**Accommodation:** Hotels concentrated along Marine Drive (beach-facing) and VIP Road. Marine Drive rooms command premium for sea views; VIP Road is quieter and slightly cheaper.

**Local auto/rickshaw:** Negotiate fares before getting in. ₹50–100 within town. Avoid drivers who approach you near the temple — standard overcharging zone.

**Medical:** SCB Medical College Hospital is 60 km away in Cuttack. District Headquarters Hospital in Puri for emergencies. For mild issues: pharmacies open until 10 PM near Swargadwar.
