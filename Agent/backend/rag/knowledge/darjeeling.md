# Darjeeling Travel Knowledge

## Routing

**Standard routing: NJP (New Jalpaiguri) → Darjeeling**

NJP railway station and Bagdogra Airport share the same hill road to Darjeeling — both 80 km away. The drive on NH10/NH55 (Hill Cart Road) involves continuous hairpin bends from Siliguri onward; carry motion sickness medication for children.

Driving times:
- NJP / Bagdogra → Darjeeling: 3–4 hours via NH10/NH55 (80 km)
- Gangtok → Darjeeling: 4–5 hours (100 km via NH10 and state roads)
- Kalimpong → Darjeeling: 2–2.5 hours (50 km)
- Siliguri → Darjeeling: 3–4 hours (73 km via NH10)

**Alternative routing:** The DHR toy train from NJP takes 7–8 hours — a heritage experience, not a transit option. The Darjeeling–Ghum 2-hour joyride (see below) is the practical tourist format.

## Darjeeling Town & Tiger Hill

**Key places:** Tiger Hill (11 km), Batasia Loop, Observatory Hill (Mahakal Temple), Himalayan Mountaineering Institute (HMI) + Zoo, Mall Road

**Tiger Hill sunrise:**
- Entry: ₹50/person; shared jeep ₹200–300/seat, private ₹1,200–1,500 — book through hotel or GTA taxi stand the evening before
- Departure 4–4:30 AM; viewpoint flat once arrived — accessible for elderly by jeep. Temperature 5–12°C even in summer; warm layers essential for all ages. No barriers at the railing — hold toddlers at all times.
- Kanchenjunga visible October–May; cloud-obscured June–September. Jeep road can block in landslides July–September.

**Batasia Loop (DHR Toy Train Joyride):**
- Entry: ₹50 for war memorial garden; toy train joyride (Darjeeling–Ghum, ~2 hours) ₹1,000–1,500/person — book at Darjeeling station or irctc.co.in
- Enclosed seated ride — suitable for elderly and toddlers. Batasia garden is flat and stroller-accessible.
- Afternoon fog often cancels views; morning runs preferred. Landslide disruptions are frequent — check status before planning.

**HMI + Padmaja Naidu Himalayan Zoo:**
- Entry: ₹100/Indians, ₹200/foreigners (combined ticket)
- Graded campus paths — manageable for elderly. Zoo has some slopes but largely accessible. Red pandas are reliable child-friendly attraction (ages 3–10). Toddlers in carrier manageable.
- Best time: 9 AM–noon; closed Tuesdays

**Trust issue — toy train "sold out" agents:**
- Agents near Mall Road claim DHR joyride tickets are sold out and sell at ₹2,000–3,000. Tickets are rarely sold out except during October Puja holidays.
- Recommended approach: Buy directly at Darjeeling railway station counter the evening before or check irctc.co.in. Never buy from street agents.

**Darjeeling Town food:**
- Local dishes: Momos (steamed or fried dumplings — pork, chicken, veg; ₹60–120/plate), Thukpa (Tibetan noodle soup ₹80–120), Sel Roti (Nepali sweet rice-flour ring, ₹15–20/piece at roadside stalls), Aloo Dum (Nepali spiced potato curry)
- For toddlers and young kids: Veg steamed momos are soft and well-tolerated from 18 months+; plain Thukpa broth with noodles available on request at most Tibetan restaurants
- Vegetarian availability: Moderate — pork momos are the default; vegetarian versions exist at all restaurants but must be specified clearly

## Tea Gardens & Outer Darjeeling

**Key places:** Happy Valley Tea Estate (3 km), Ghum Monastery (8 km), Rock Garden/Barbotey (10 km), Peace Pagoda

**Happy Valley Tea Estate:**
- Entry: Free; guided factory tour ₹100/person (active March–November; factory closed December–February)
- Uneven hillside terrain — strollers impractical; elderly should stay on the lower path only; toddlers need to be carried during the factory tour. Active during first flush (March–May) and second flush (June–August).
- Best time: 7–9 AM to see active leaf plucking

**Ghum Monastery (Yiga Choeling):**
- Entry: Free (donation expected)
- Flat courtyard and interior — accessible for strollers and elderly. Active monastery (1850s); maintain silence inside and ask at entrance whether a prayer session is in progress.

**Trust issue — "free tea tasting" upsell at estate gates:**
- Men at tea estate gates and on the hill road offer free tastings that end with aggressive ₹1,500–4,000 box upsells; refusal triggers confrontation.
- Recommended approach: The official Happy Valley factory tour is legitimate. For buying tea, use the Tea Board of India outlet on Nehru Road, Darjeeling town — fixed government grades and prices (first flush quality: ₹400–800/100g). Do not buy from roadside tasting stalls.

**Tea Garden / Outer food:**
- Happy Valley has no stall — carry snacks from town. Rock Garden area has roadside momos and tea (₹30–80).
- For toddlers: Carry from Darjeeling town; no toddler-specific options at outer sites.
- Vegetarian availability: Roadside stalls near outer sites are predominantly non-vegetarian; confirm before ordering momos.

## General Darjeeling Notes

**Best time to visit:**
- March–May: Best overall. First flush season, clear skies, Kanchenjunga visible most mornings. Temperatures 10–18°C.
- October–November: Post-monsoon clarity, excellent views. October Puja period brings surge crowds — book 3–4 weeks ahead.
- June–September: Heavy monsoon. NH10 landslides cause multi-hour or multi-day blockages. Toy train cancellations common. Tiger Hill views obscured. Not recommended for families or first visits.
- December–February: Cold (2–8°C), possible frost. Clear Kanchenjunga views. Not ideal for toddlers or elderly sensitive to cold.

**Transport pricing:**
- GTA taxis cover all hill routes — no meters, all prices negotiated. Shared jeep within town: ₹15–30/seat. Full-day sightseeing cab: ₹1,500–2,500.
- NJP → Darjeeling: ₹250–300/person shared jeep; ₹2,000–2,500 private cab

**Dietary notes:**
- Nepali-Tibetan food culture; pork and chicken dominate. Vegetarians must specify at every meal — default dishes are non-vegetarian.
- Tea sold at roadside stalls is typically low-grade dust; buy from Tea Board outlet or estate counters for genuine Darjeeling tea.

**Medical:** Darjeeling District Hospital for routine cases. Specialist/emergency care: NBMCH (North Bengal Medical College and Hospital), Siliguri — 80 km, 3–4 hours. Altitude is 2,050m — allow 24 hours of light activity before strenuous outings, especially for elderly and toddlers from the plains.
