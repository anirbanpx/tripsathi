# Rishikesh Travel Knowledge

## Routing

**Standard routing: Delhi → Haridwar → Rishikesh**

Haridwar is a natural half-day stop — the evening Ganga Aarti at Har Ki Pauri is 45 minutes from Rishikesh and worth attending en route. Going directly to Rishikesh is possible but the final stretch on NH7 into town congests heavily after 4 PM.

Driving times:
- Delhi → Haridwar: 4.5–5.5 hours via NH334 (Delhi–Haridwar Highway) through Meerut and Roorkee
- Haridwar → Rishikesh: 45 min–1 hour via NH7 (25 km; congestion-dependent)
- Rishikesh → Shivpuri rafting base: 30 min (17 km upstream on NH7)
- Rishikesh → Neelkanth Mahadev Temple: 1.5 hours (32 km on narrow forest road)

**Alternative routing:** Fly into Jolly Grant Airport (Dehradun, 35 km away) — ₹4,000–9,000 one-way from Delhi; taxi to Rishikesh ₹800–1,000, ~1 hour. Better option for elderly or toddler families avoiding a 6–7 hour road journey.

## Ram Jhula & Ghat Area

**Key places:** Triveni Ghat, Parmarth Niketan, Ram Jhula (Shivanand Jhula), Beatles Ashram, Tapovan / Laxman Jhula market area

**Triveni Ghat Aarti:**
- Entry: Free; Parmarth Niketan seated section requires free prior registration at their desk
- Evening aarti at 6 PM (6:30 PM winter) — suitable for all ages including toddlers and elderly. Riverfront steps are uneven; elderly need assistance. Keep toddlers in a carrier during the ceremony — crowd surges at the close.
- No seasonal closure; Kartik Purnima (Oct–Nov) brings very dense crowds

**Beatles Ashram (Maharishi Mahesh Yogi Ashram / Chaurasi Kutia):**
- Entry: ₹150/adult Indians, ₹600/foreigners; ₹100 camera fee
- Forested campus with 20–30 min of uneven stone paths — not suitable for prams or strollers; elderly need steady footing. Toddlers must be carried.
- Best time: 8–11 AM (cooler, less crowded)
- Closed Mondays

**Trust issue — unsolicited ghat-side puja touts:**
- Men near Triveni Ghat and Ram Jhula claim to be "ashram staff" registering names for prayer, then perform an unrequested puja and demand ₹500–2,000.
- Recommended approach: No legitimate ashram registers pilgrims on the ghat. Decline all unsolicited approaches. For a real puja, walk directly to Parmarth Niketan's main reception and ask there.

**Ram Jhula / Ghat area food:**
- Rishikesh is a no-meat, no-alcohol zone by municipal rule — all restaurants are vegetarian throughout the town.
- Local dishes: Aloo Puri (fried bread with spiced potato), Chhole Bhature, Ashram Thali at Parmarth Niketan dining hall (₹80–150: dal, sabzi, roti, rice), Malai Lassi at Ram Jhula stalls
- For toddlers and young kids: Parmarth Niketan thali dal and plain rice is mild and reliable; banana lassi widely available
- Vegetarian availability: Entirely vegetarian by default; egg availability varies — many places are "pure veg" (no eggs)

## Shivpuri Adventure Zone

**Key places:** Shivpuri rafting camps, Marine Drive launch point, Jumpin Heights bungee (Mohan Chatti, 83m — Asia's highest fixed bungee)

**River rafting grades and suitability:**
- Brahmpuri → Rishikesh (9 km, Grade 1–2): ₹400–600/person — families with kids aged 8+, first-timers; not for toddlers, children under 8, or elderly with back/joint issues
- Shivpuri → Rishikesh (16 km, Grade 3–4): ₹800–1,200/person — minimum age 14 enforced by reputable operators
- Marine Drive → Rishikesh (26 km, Grade 4–5): ₹1,200–1,800/person — experienced swimmers only
- Rafting is closed district-wide July to mid-September (SDRF seasonal ban)

**Trust issue — unlicensed rafting operators on Mall Road:**
- Agents near Ram Jhula and Laxman Jhula undercut licensed operators at ₹200–400/person. These operators routinely skip life jacket checks, overload rafts, and have no rescue protocol.
- Recommended approach: Book only with UTDB-registered operators; their counters display a Uttarakhand Tourism certification sticker. Inspect life jackets before boarding — they should be ISRS-certified, not foam scraps. Reputable direct operators at Shivpuri: Aquaterra Adventures, Red Chilli Adventure, Garhwal Himalayan Exploration.

**Shivpuri food:**
- Camp kitchens serve dal, rice, sabzi — included in most rafting packages; quality varies by operator
- For toddlers: Carry food from Rishikesh town; camp kitchens have no toddler-specific options
- Vegetarian availability: All rafting camp kitchens in the Rishikesh corridor are vegetarian

## General Rishikesh Notes

**Best time to visit:**
- February–April: Best overall. Rafting fully open, temperatures 15–28°C. March–April ideal for families and elderly.
- May–June: Hot (35–40°C) but rafting still running pre-monsoon. Crowds build; book 2–3 weeks ahead.
- July–mid-September: Monsoon. Rafting suspended (typically June 15–September 15 by official order). Neelkanth road prone to landslides. Yoga and ashram stays unaffected. Not for adventure travellers.
- October–January: Best post-monsoon clarity; rafting resumes in September–October. December–January cold (5–15°C) but dry. Peak yoga retreat and wellness tourism season.

**Transport pricing:**
- No metered autos — negotiate upfront. Ram Jhula to Laxman Jhula area: ₹60–80. Within town: ₹50–100.
- Ola available in Rishikesh (limited) — pre-book for airport transfers; otherwise rely on local taxis.
- Rishikesh → Haridwar: ₹400–500/seat shared cab (from bus stand); ₹700–900 private taxi

**Dietary notes:**
- Rishikesh is legally meat-free and alcohol-free within municipal limits. Tapovan and Laxman Jhula area has Israeli and Western cafés serving eggs, hummus, and global vegetarian fare at ₹150–400/item.
- No allergy risk from meat cross-contamination as it is absent from the entire food supply within town.

**Medical:** AIIMS Rishikesh (All India Institute of Medical Sciences, ~5 km from Ram Jhula) has 24-hour emergency and is the strongest trauma and cardiac facility in the Garhwal region. For rafting injuries, first aid is available at Shivpuri camp zone; serious cases are transferred to AIIMS Rishikesh.
