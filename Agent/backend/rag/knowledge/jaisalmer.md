# Jaisalmer Travel Knowledge

## Routing

**Standard routing: Jodhpur → Jaisalmer**

Jaisalmer is almost always the western terminus of a Rajasthan circuit — nothing major lies further west. Jodhpur is the natural staging point; NH25 is entirely flat highway through open desert with no difficult sections.

Driving times:
- Jodhpur → Jaisalmer: 4–4.5 hours via NH25 (290 km)
- Jaipur → Jaisalmer: 9–10 hours via NH11 through Bikaner or via NH62 through Jodhpur — break with an overnight in Jodhpur
- Jaisalmer town → Sam Sand Dunes: 45 min (42 km via Sam Road/SH40)
- Jaisalmer town → Khuri Dunes: 1 hour (48 km, narrow state road)

**Alternative routing:** Jaisalmer Airport has flights from Delhi (1.5–2 hours, ₹4,000–10,000 one-way) on limited, seasonally variable schedules — confirm before planning. Best option for elderly and toddler families avoiding a 9-hour road journey.

## Jaisalmer Fort & Old City

**Key places:** Sonar Quila (Golden Fort), Patwon ki Haveli, Salim Singh ki Haveli, Gadisar Lake

**Jaisalmer Fort:**
- Entry: Free to fort precincts; Fort Palace Museum ₹200/Indians, ₹500/foreigners; audio guide ₹150
- Living settlement with cobblestone lanes — strollers cannot navigate; carry toddlers. Elderly can access the main Chowk and ground-floor shops but upper ramparts have steep uneven stairs.
- Best time: 7–9 AM; rampart sunset viable October–March
- Desert Festival (February): fort lanes reach capacity — not suitable for toddlers or elderly

**Patwon ki Haveli:**
- Entry: ₹50/Indians
- Five-storey haveli with steep narrow internal stairs — not suitable for toddlers in arms or elderly with knee/joint issues. Ground floor accessible and gives a representative view.
- Best time: 9–11 AM before stone interiors heat up

**Gadisar Lake:**
- Entry: Free; boat ride ₹50–100/person
- Flat paved surrounds — accessible for strollers and elderly. Best October–February for migratory birds; afternoons get dusty winds in summer.

**Trust issue — "free" camel ride conversion near fort gates:**
- Boys near fort gates and haveli entrances offer a "free" or ₹50 short ride. Once on the camel, the price is renegotiated to ₹500–1,500. Some pull children onto camels without consent before quoting a price.
- Recommended approach: There is no free camel ride in Jaisalmer. Book through your hotel or an RTDC-registered operator at Sam. Refuse all camel/horse approaches in fort lanes.

**Fort & Old City food:**
- Local dishes: Dal Baati Churma (₹150–250 at fort restaurants), Ker Sangri (wild desert beans and berries stir-fried with spices — unique to the Thar region), Bajra Roti with Lehsun Chutney (millet flatbread with garlic chutney), Makhaniya Lassi (thick butter-lassi)
- For toddlers and young kids: Plain dal and roti on request at all fort restaurants; Ker Sangri is too tart for young children
- Vegetarian availability: Strong throughout fort and old city. Laal Maas (mutton) available at non-veg restaurants outside the fort walls.

## Sam Sand Dunes & Desert Camps

**Key places:** Sam Sand Dunes (42 km), Khuri Dunes (quieter alternative, 48 km)

**Sam Sand Dunes:**
- Entry: Free; camel safari ₹300–500/person (30-min); jeep safari ₹800–1,200
- Toddlers: dunes in a carrier, not on a camel independently. Camel rides not suitable for toddlers under 4 or elderly with back/spine conditions.
- Best time: 4:30–6:30 PM for sunset. Avoid 11 AM–3 PM — sand surface exceeds 55°C in summer.
- October–February optimal

**Trust issue — luxury camp bait-and-switch:**
- Agents and online aggregators sell "Swiss tent" or "luxury camp" packages (₹3,000–8,000/person) using polished stock photos. On arrival, the actual camp is basic canvas tents with shared bathrooms and no resemblance to the booking photos. This is the single most consistent complaint from Jaisalmer overnight visitors.
- Recommended approach: Book only from camps with recent (last 6 months) verified Google or TripAdvisor reviews containing actual guest photos. Call the camp directly; ask for a WhatsApp video of the current tent interior. Avoid aggregator packages that do not name the specific camp.

**Sam / Camp food:**
- Camp dinners included in most packages — buffet of Dal Baati, sabzi, roti, rice. Quality varies widely by camp tier.
- For toddlers and young kids: Plain dal and roti available at all camp kitchens; carry toddler snacks for the 45-min drive each way
- Vegetarian availability: All camp kitchens are vegetarian or offer full vegetarian menus

## General Jaisalmer Notes

**Best time to visit:**
- October–February: Best overall. Temperatures 8–28°C; dunes and fort viable all day. January nights drop to 4–7°C — warm layers essential for overnight camp stays.
- March–April: Comfortable through March. Dust storms (andhi) begin in April — can reduce Sam visibility to near-zero without warning.
- May–September: May–June temperatures reach 45–48°C; outdoor activity impossible 10 AM–5 PM. Jaisalmer gets minimal monsoon rain, but heat alone makes family or elderly visits inadvisable.

**Transport pricing:**
- No Ola or Uber in Jaisalmer — negotiate all transport locally.
- Auto-rickshaws within town: ₹60–100; railway station to fort ~₹80–100
- Private cab to Sam (full day, dunes + sunset + return): ₹1,200–1,800
- Shared jeep to Sam: ₹80–100/seat (from near Hanuman Circle)

**Dietary notes:**
- Desert cuisine relies on dried and preserved ingredients — fresh produce is limited in restaurants outside the city core. Spice levels are high by default; request "kam mirch" (less chilli) for children.
- Vegetarian options: Widely available; fort area is predominantly vegetarian.

**Medical:** Jaisalmer District Government Hospital handles basic emergencies with limited specialist capacity. Nearest quality hospital: Jodhpur (~290 km, 4 hours) — Dr. S.N. Medical College (government) or Goyal Hospital (private). For cardiac, trauma, or paediatric emergencies, plan for stabilisation and transfer to Jodhpur. Carry a basic travel first-aid kit for overnight desert camps.
