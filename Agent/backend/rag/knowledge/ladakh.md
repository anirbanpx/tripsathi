# Ladakh Travel Knowledge

## Routing

**Standard routing: Fly to Leh → 2 days acclimatisation → Nubra Valley → Pangong Lake → Leh departure**

Mandatory acclimatisation first — no exceptions:
- Leh sits at 3,500m. Flying in from sea level means your body needs 2 full days of rest before any excursion.
- Day 1 in Leh: arrive, rest, short walk only. Do NOT drive to any high-altitude site same day.
- Day 2 in Leh: light local sightseeing (Shanti Stupa, Leh Palace, Leh market) — nothing above 3,700m.
- Only from Day 3 onwards: proceed to Nubra Valley or Pangong Lake.

Driving times (from Leh):
- Leh → Nubra Valley (via Khardung La): 4–5 hours (100 km; Khardung La at 5,359m)
- Leh → Pangong Lake (via Chang La): 4.5–5 hours (150 km; Chang La at 5,360m)
- Leh → Tso Moriri: 6–7 hours (240 km; more remote, fewer crowds)

**Alternative routing: Manali → Leh highway (2 days overland)**
- Manali to Leh via NH 3: 2-day drive with overnight halt at Jispa or Sarchu (4,200m).
- Gradual altitude gain is actually better for acclimatisation than flying — body adjusts progressively.
- Road open: roughly June to mid-October (snow blocks it rest of year). Check BSNL road status or local reports before departure.
- Not recommended for elderly or toddlers — 2 rough days at altitude with limited medical access.

**Srinagar → Leh highway (NH 1):** 1-day drive (434 km). Road opens earlier (May) than Manali highway. More military checkpoints; carry ID.

## Altitude Sickness — Critical Warning

**HARD contra-indication:** Toddlers (under 3) and elderly with any cardiac, respiratory, or blood pressure condition should NOT travel above 3,500m. This is a genuine medical risk, not a comfort consideration.

- Acute Mountain Sickness (AMS) symptoms: headache, nausea, dizziness, breathlessness.
- Standard protocol if AMS occurs: descend immediately. Do not ascend further.
- Diamox (acetazolamide) is commonly used prophylactically — consult a doctor before the trip; not suitable for children without medical advice.
- Khardung La (5,359m) and Chang La (5,360m) passes: healthy adults can visit briefly but should minimise time at the top (30 min max).
- Pregnant women: avoid travel above 3,500m.
- Children ages 3–12: can travel to Leh (3,500m) if fully healthy but monitor closely; any AMS symptom = descend.

## Permits

**Inner Line Permit (ILP):** Required for Indian citizens to visit restricted areas.
- Nubra Valley: requires ILP
- Pangong Lake: requires ILP
- Tso Moriri: requires ILP
- Dah-Hanu (Aryan Valley): requires ILP
- Leh town and local monasteries: no permit needed

**How to get ILP:**
- Online: ladakhtourism.gov.in (e--permits); apply 2–3 days before travel.
- In-person: DC Office, Leh — morning hours, expect queues.
- Cost: ₹50–200/person depending on area; minimal.
- Your taxi/tour operator will usually handle this for you if booked through them — confirm this explicitly.

**Foreign nationals:** Require Protected Area Permit (PAP) for restricted zones; apply at Deputy Commissioner's office in Leh with passport and Indian visa copy.

## Nubra Valley

**Key places:** Diskit Monastery, Hunder Sand Dunes (double-humped Bactrian camels), Panamik hot springs

**Diskit Monastery:**
- Entry: ₹20. 32-metre Maitreya Buddha statue (inaugurated by Dalai Lama).
- Moderate stair climb — doable for elderly who are fit; skip for toddlers.
- Morning prayers at 6–7 AM — respectful visitors welcome.

**Hunder Sand Dunes:**
- Bactrian camel rides: ₹500–700 for 20–30 minutes. Suitable for all ages including young kids (seated with adult).
- Sand dunes are modest — nothing like Jaisalmer's Sam Dunes.

**Nubra accommodation:** Stay in Hunder or Diskit village. Guesthouses ₹1,500–3,000/night; small camps ₹2,500–4,000. Facilities are basic but clean.

## Pangong Lake

- Famous for 3 Idiots filming location (though that was a different part of the lake).
- Stunning blue water at 4,350m altitude — arrives suddenly after a long mountain drive.
- **No swimming** — water is extremely cold (near freezing) year-round.
- Tented camps along the southern shore (Spangmik, Man, Merak): ₹2,000–4,000/night. No ATMs — carry all cash from Leh.
- Sunrise (6–7 AM) is exceptional for photography; most tourists leave by 11 AM.
- Toilets in camps are basic; some camps now have attached western toilets — confirm before booking.

## Monastery Circuit

**Thiksey Monastery:**
- Entry: ₹50. 12-storey monastery resembling Potala Palace, Lhasa.
- Morning prayers: 6–7 AM — most atmospheric time to visit.
- 18km from Leh; easy half-day trip.

**Hemis Monastery:**
- Entry: ₹100. Largest and wealthiest monastery in Ladakh.
- Annual Hemis Festival (Jul): major draw with masked dances (Cham) — book accommodation months in advance for festival dates.
- 40km from Leh; combine with Thiksey on same day.

**Diskit Monastery:** (covered under Nubra Valley above)

## General Ladakh Notes

**Best time to visit:**
- Jun–Sep: Only viable window for road access and all sites. Sep is ideal — crowds thin, weather stable, passes open.
- Jul–Aug: Peak season; accommodation fills up, prices rise 20–30%, Pangong camps often fully booked — reserve 4–6 weeks ahead.
- Oct–May: Manali-Leh highway closed. Leh accessible only by flight. Some travellers do winter Leh for frozen river (Chadar) trek — extreme cold, not for families.

**Transport:**
- Hire a local taxi for all excursions — Leh taxi union operates fixed-rate cards. Leh to Pangong round trip: ₹5,000–6,500 for a Sumo/Innova.
- Do not attempt high-altitude passes in a small hatchback. Innova, Sumo, or Bolero preferred.
- Bikes (Royal Enfield): popular but only for experienced riders with proper riding gear and AMS awareness.

**Food:**
- Leh town has good cafes and restaurants serving Tibetan, Indian, and continental food (Bon Appetit, Gesmo, La Pizzeria are reliable).
- Remote areas (Pangong, Nubra, Tso Moriri): food available only at camps — limited menu, often rice + dal + sabzi. Carry snacks, biscuits, and glucose tablets.
- For kids: Maggi noodles and rice are available everywhere. Spice levels low in Ladakhi food by default.

**Medical:** Carry a personal first aid kit including Diamox, ORS, paracetamol, and altitude sickness medication. Nearest hospital with ICU: SNM Hospital, Leh. Above 4,000m: nearest medical help is Leh (hours away). Portable oxygen cylinders available for rent in Leh (₹200–400/day) — worth carrying for elderly travellers.
