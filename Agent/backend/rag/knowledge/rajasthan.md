# Rajasthan Travel Knowledge

## Routing

**Standard routing: Jaipur → Jodhpur → Jaisalmer → Udaipur (→ Jaipur or Ahmedabad for departure)**

This west-to-east arc is strongly preferred because:
- Jaipur (closest to Delhi) is the natural entry point from the north
- Jaisalmer in the middle of the trip means the long desert drive is broken up
- Udaipur at the end is the most scenic and relaxed — good finale before flying out
- Ahmedabad (6h from Udaipur) is an alternative exit if flying south

Driving times:
- Jaipur → Jodhpur: 5–6 hours (NH 58)
- Jodhpur → Jaisalmer: 4.5–5 hours (NH 125)
- Jaisalmer → Udaipur: 7–8 hours (long day; break at Jodhpur is better)
- Udaipur → Jaipur: 6–7 hours

**Alternative routing: Delhi → Jaipur → Pushkar → Jodhpur → Udaipur (skip Jaisalmer)**
Suitable for families with toddlers or elderly who cannot handle the extreme desert heat or long Jaisalmer drive.

## Jaipur

**Key places:** Amber Fort, City Palace, Jantar Mantar, Hawa Mahal, Nahargarh Fort

**Amber Fort:**
- Entry: ₹200/adult (Indian), ₹100/child under 12. Light and sound show evenings ₹295/adult.
- Elephant rides: now officially discontinued at many points due to animal welfare issues — skip. Use the free jeep service (included in entry) up the ramp.
- Fort interior involves significant stair climbing — not suitable for strollers or elderly with mobility issues.
- Best visited 8–10 AM before tour groups arrive; extremely hot by noon.

**Hawa Mahal:** Entry ₹50 (Indian). Exterior photography from street is free — most visitors do this rather than climb inside. Interior climb is steep, not stroller-accessible.

**City Palace:** Entry ₹200 (Indian). Museum inside; flat courtyards suitable for all groups.

**Jaipur hotel location:**
- Stay within the walled city (old city) or MI Road area for easy access to all forts. Hotels near Bani Park or C-Scheme are 6–8 km from main sites — you'll spend significantly on transport.

## Jodhpur

**Key places:** Mehrangarh Fort, Jaswant Thada, Clock Tower market (Sardar Bazaar), Umaid Bhawan Palace

**Mehrangarh Fort:**
- Entry: ₹600/adult (Indian), ₹300/child — includes audio guide. One of the best-maintained forts in India.
- Lift available from ground level to one floor; upper ramparts require stairs.
- Audio guide in Hindi/English — very good for self-paced touring. Duration: 2–3 hours.
- Suitable for elderly if they skip the uppermost rampart. Not suitable for strollers throughout.

**Jodhpur hotel location:** Blue City area near the fort (Navchokiya) for best atmosphere; budget stays in this zone ₹1,500–3,000/night. Heritage havelis ₹4,000–8,000.

## Jaisalmer

**Key places:** Jaisalmer Fort (living fort, people still reside inside), Patwon Ki Haveli, Sam Sand Dunes, Khuri Dunes

**Camel safari — trust issues:**
- Sam Sand Dunes (45 km from Jaisalmer): most popular, heavily commercialised. Touts at the Sam gate charge ₹500–800 for 30-min camel rides — overpriced and rushed.
- Recommended approach: book a full desert camp package through your hotel or a verified operator — this includes camel ride + overnight desert camp + meals. Price: ₹3,000–6,000/person.
- Reputable operators: Thar Desert Camp (Sam), Royal Desert Camp (Khuri — quieter alternative).
- Khuri (50 km from Jaisalmer): fewer tourists, more authentic, no generators-and-DJ problem that Sam Sand Dunes has on peak nights.
- Age suitability for camel rides: 4+ years with adult; elderly with back problems should skip.
- Overnight desert camp: tented accommodation ₹2,000–4,000/night. Not suitable for toddlers under 2 — no proper facilities, cold nights (15°C) in winter, very hot in summer.

**Jaisalmer Fort:**
- Still a living settlement — shops, restaurants, guesthouses inside.
- Entry to ramparts ₹100 (Indian). Interior streets are uneven cobblestone — not stroller-accessible.

## Udaipur

**Key places:** Lake Pichola, City Palace, Jagdish Temple, Saheliyon Ki Bari, Monsoon Palace

**Lake Pichola boat ride:**
- Government boats: ₹400/adult (sunset cruise, 1 hour). Timings: roughly 10 AM–5:30 PM; sunset slot most popular.
- Private boat hire: ₹1,500–2,000 for 45 min. Worth it for photos without the crowd.
- Boats are wooden with low railings — keep toddlers seated and supervised throughout.
- Book same-day at the City Palace boat jetty; no advance booking usually required except peak season (Dec–Jan).

**Udaipur hotel location:**
- Old city (Lal Ghat, Gangaur Ghat) gives walking access to City Palace and lake ghats. Heritage havelis here: ₹2,500–6,000/night.
- Fateh Sagar Lake area is quieter but 3 km from main sites — requires cab.
- Hotels on the highway (Bedla Road) are significantly cheaper but 8+ km from old city — not worth it for a short stay.

## Pushkar (Day trip or 1-night add-on from Jaipur/Ajmer)

- Car-free sacred town; vehicles park outside. Walk-in only.
- Brahma Temple (one of very few in India): free entry, dress code enforced (remove shoes, cover head).
- Pushkar Lake: no swimming, no leather items near the lake (sacred site).
- Camel fair (Kartik Purnima, Oct–Nov): extremely crowded, accommodation doubles in price — book 3 months ahead.
- Good for all groups including elderly (flat town, gentle pace). Not suitable for strollers on narrow lanes.

## General Rajasthan Notes

**Best time to visit:**
- Oct–Mar: Ideal. Pleasant days (20–30°C), cold nights in Dec–Jan (5–10°C in Jaisalmer desert).
- Apr–Jun: AVOID with toddlers, elderly, or anyone with cardiac/respiratory conditions. Jaisalmer and Jodhpur hit 45°C+. Heat exhaustion risk is serious.
- Jul–Sep: Monsoon. Jaipur and Udaipur receive moderate rain; Jaisalmer gets very little. Forts can be slippery; some desert camp operators close.

**Palace hotels vs heritage havelis:**
- Palace hotels (Taj, Oberoi, Samode): ₹8,000–25,000/night. Immaculate, family facilities, pools.
- Heritage havelis (boutique): ₹2,500–6,000/night. More authentic feel, variable service, often no pool.
- For families with young kids: palace hotels worth the premium for hygiene, kids' facilities, and reliable food.

**Transport pricing:**
- Intercity cabs (AC Innova for family of 4-5): ₹12–15/km. Book via MakeMyTrip, hotel, or local operators.
- Auto-rickshaws in Jaipur, Jodhpur, Udaipur: negotiate strictly; meters are widely ignored. Ola available in Jaipur.

**Dietary notes:**
- Rajasthani food is largely vegetarian (dal baati churma, gatte ki sabzi, ker sangri). Good for vegetarian travellers.
- Kids' food: roti, rice, dal, plain paneer available everywhere.
- Jaisalmer restaurants cater heavily to tourists — menus have continental options.

**Medical:** Carry ORS, sunscreen SPF 50+, and loose cotton clothing for heat. Nearest major hospitals: SMS Hospital Jaipur, AIIMS Jodhpur. In remote desert areas (Khuri, Sam), medical facilities are 50+ km away.
