# Kerala Travel Knowledge

## Routing

**Standard routing: Kochi → Munnar → Alleppey (→ Kochi for departure)**

Munnar before Alleppey is strongly recommended because:
- Munnar is cooler (1600m altitude) — do it early before heat fatigue sets in
- Alleppey backwaters are best experienced at the end as a relaxing finish
- Kochi serves as gateway on both ends (international/domestic airport)
- Reverse routing (Alleppey first) means ending in cool hills, then rushing back to Kochi for flight — logistically harder

Driving times:
- Kochi → Munnar: 3.5–4 hours (NH 183, ghat section from Adimali)
- Munnar → Alleppey: 4–5 hours (via Kothamangalam or Ernakulam)
- Alleppey → Kochi Airport: 1.5 hours

**Alternative routing: Wayanad instead of Munnar**
Wayanad (Kozhikode gateway) suits families who want wildlife + plantation without steep ghat roads. Wayanad → Kochi is 4–5 hours. Less popular but lower altitude = easier travel with kids.

## Munnar

**Key places:** Tea Museum, Eravikulam National Park, Mattupetty Dam, Echo Point, Top Station

**Eravikulam National Park (Rajamalai):**
- Entry: ₹100/adult, ₹50/child. Shuttle bus from gate to viewpoint.
- The walk to the Nilgiri Tahr viewpoint is 3–4 km on uneven mountain terrain.
- Unsuitable for children under 5 years: steep, narrow paths, no stroller access.
- Unsuitable for elderly or mobility-limited travellers: elevation, uneven surface, no rest stops.
- Park is closed during Nilgiri Tahr calving season (Feb–Mar).
- Best visited 7–9 AM before crowds.

**Mattupetty Dam:** Easy walk, flat terrain. Suitable for all groups including toddlers and elderly. Pedal boats available (₹150–200/30 min).

**Tea estates:** Most allow walkthroughs; request guide for free estate walk at most resorts.

**Munnar food:**
- Most restaurants serve rice + curry, dosa, appam, puttu.
- For toddlers and young kids: idli (soft rice cakes), plain appam, puttu with banana — available at all local restaurants. Request "no spice" (no chilli, no pepper).
- Restaurants near town: Rapsy Restaurant (budget), SN Restaurant (good thali).
- Note: Munnar restaurants close early (~9:30 PM); book dinner by 8:30 PM.

## Alleppey (Alappuzha)

**Key experiences:** Houseboat (kettuvalam), canoe/kayak rides, Alleppey beach, Krishnapuram Palace.

**Houseboat booking — critical trust issue:**
- Cold-approach operators at KSRTC bus stand and jetty area inflate prices by 30–50% and may provide substandard boats.
- Recommended approach: book houseboat through your hotel or a verified aggregator (Kerala Tourism-licensed operators). Your hotel front desk can call trusted operators they have relationships with.
- Premium operators: Spice Routes Leisure (premium), Vasudeva Vilasam (mid-range). Both Kerala-Tourism certified.
- Price guide: Standard 1-bedroom houseboat, overnight: ₹6,000–9,000. Premium: ₹12,000–18,000. All-inclusive (meals onboard).
- Overnight houseboat with toddlers (under 2): not recommended — no railings on deck, open water, limited bathroom facilities. Day houseboat (4–6 hrs) is safer for families with very young kids.

**Alleppey hotel location:**
- Hotels near the backwater junction (South Alleppey, near KSRTC) give direct boat access.
- Hotels on the national highway (NH 66) or near Alleppey beach are 3–5 km from the backwater network — you'll need a hired boat to reach houseboats, adding cost and time.
- Recommended area: Finishing Point Road or near Alleppey boat jetty for best access.

**Canoe/kayak rides:** 2–3 hour village backwater tours, ₹500–800/person. Suitable for kids 3+. More authentic than houseboats for experiencing village life.

**Alleppey food:**
- Coconut-heavy seafood cuisine; vegetarian options in all restaurants but less variety.
- For kids: plain rice with dal (available on request), idli, dosa. Seafood restaurants usually have a "veg section."
- Thaff Restaurant: reliable veg + non-veg. Harbour Restaurant: good for seafood.

## Kochi (Cochin)

**Key places:** Fort Kochi (Chinese fishing nets, Dutch Palace, Jew Town), Marine Drive, Lulu Mall.

**Fort Kochi:**
- Chinese fishing nets: best seen at sunrise or sunset. Photography allowed.
- Mattancherry Palace (Dutch Palace): ₹5 entry. 16th-century murals. No strollers inside.
- Jew Town antique shops: good for souvenirs. Prices negotiable.
- Fort Kochi heritage walk: 2–3 hours, flat terrain, suitable for all groups.

**Kochi → Munnar transport options:**
- Private cab: ₹2,500–3,500 (AC sedan). Book via hotel or app (Ola, Uber available in Kochi city only).
- KSRTC bus: ₹120–150, 4–5 hrs. Not recommended with young kids (crowded, no AC on some routes).

## General Kerala Notes

**Best time to visit:**
- Oct–Feb: Ideal (post-monsoon, cool and clear)
- Mar–May: Hot and dry in plains; Munnar still pleasant
- Jun–Sep: Monsoon. Houseboat routes may be restricted. Eravikulam closed.

**Auto/rickshaw pricing:**
- Kochi, Alleppey: Meter auto available but often ignored; negotiate or use Ola Autos (app-based, fair rate).
- Munnar: No auto meters; negotiate fixed price before getting in. ₹50–100 for in-town trips.

**Dietary notes:**
- Most Kerala food uses coconut oil; inform restaurants if allergy.
- Vegetarian options always available; pure-veg restaurants common in Munnar town.
- Seafood is predominant in Alleppey and Kochi.

**Medical:** Carry ORS sachets and basic first aid. Nearest quality hospital from Munnar: KIMS, Ernakulam (2.5 hrs).
