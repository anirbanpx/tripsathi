# Guwahati & Northeast India Travel Knowledge

## Guwahati Overview

Gateway city to Northeast India. Most travellers base themselves in Guwahati and take day trips to Shillong, Kaziranga, or Cherrapunji.

**Key places in Guwahati:** Kamakhya Temple, Umananda Island Temple, Brahmaputra riverfront, Assam State Museum, Fancy Bazar (shopping).

## Kamakhya Temple — Detailed Operational Notes

**Location:** Nilachal Hill, 8 km west of Guwahati city. 10–15 min auto ride from Paltan Bazar.

**Significance:** One of the 51 Shakti Peethas; major pilgrimage site. Entry free for all.

**Queue system — critical information:**
- **General queue:** Free. Wait time: 2–5 hours on weekdays, 4–8 hours on weekends and festivals. Queue is single-file, crowded, no seating.
- **VIP darshan (Special Entry):** ₹500–700 per person. Skips 90% of the queue; total wait 20–45 minutes. Counter is inside the temple complex entrance, left side. Open from 7 AM.
- **VIP is strongly recommended** for families with young children, elderly, or anyone with time constraints.

**Physical access:**
- 150 steep stone steps from the base to the temple. No elevator or ramp.
- Unsuitable for elderly travellers with knee/hip issues or mobility limitations without assistance.
- Strollers cannot be taken up the steps.
- Alternative: hired human-pulled "doli" (chair carried by two people) available at the base, ₹300–500 return. Ask at the base.

**Timing:** 
- Open 8 AM – 1 PM, 2:30 PM – 5:30 PM (closed midday). 
- Best time: 8–9 AM (shorter queues, cooler).
- Avoid Ambubachi Mela dates (June) — massive pilgrimage crowds, days-long queues.

**Photography:** Not allowed inside the sanctum. Exterior and hillside views permitted.

**Dress code:** Shoulders and knees covered. Temple provides cloth wraps at entrance (free).

## Shillong Day Trip — Timing is Critical

**Distance:** Guwahati to Shillong: 100 km. Actual drive time: **3 to 3.5 hours each way** on NH6 due to traffic, especially at Byrnihat junction and Jorabat.

**Why timing matters:**
- Leaving Guwahati at 9–10 AM means arriving Shillong at 12:30–1:30 PM — only 3–4 hours of sightseeing before needing to turn back.
- Leaving Guwahati by **7 AM** is the standard recommendation: arrive by 10 AM, full day (6+ hours) in Shillong, depart by 4 PM, back by 7–7:30 PM.
- Leaving Shillong after 5 PM means arriving Guwahati at 9–10 PM. Night driving on NH6 is risky — heavy truck traffic and fog patches in winter.
- **Do not plan late dinner in Shillong unless staying overnight.**

**Key Shillong places:** Ward's Lake (₹20 entry, rowboats), Police Bazar (shopping), Shillong Peak (views, ₹50 entry), Don Bosco Museum (cultural, 2–3 hrs), Elephant Falls (3-tier waterfall, 20 min from city).

**Cherrapunji from Shillong:** Add 60 km extra each way. Only feasible as separate day trip or overnight; not combinable with Shillong in a single day from Guwahati.

**Transport options:**
- Private cab Guwahati → Shillong → Guwahati: ₹2,500–3,500. Book previous evening via hotel.
- Shared sumo (jeep): ₹200–250 one way from Paltan Bazar stand. No return time control — not recommended for families.

## Brahmaputra River

**Public ferry (government):**
- ₹10–30 per person, depending on route. 
- Guwahati to Umananda Island: ₹15 return. Runs every 30 min, 8 AM – 5 PM.
- Crossing time: 15–20 min. Basic wooden/steel boats, no AC, no reserved seating.
- Suitable for short hops. Not comfortable for long rides or in rough current season (June–Sep).

**Private cruise / leisure boat:**
- ₹800–2,000 per person for 2–3 hour sunset/dinner cruise.
- Air-conditioned boats with meals, deck seating, cultural programs on some.
- Book via hotel or through Assam Tourism office at MG Road.
- Best experience for families, elderly, or tourists wanting comfort.

**Brahmaputra floods:** June–September: river levels rise significantly. Check local conditions; some island temple access may be restricted.

## Kaziranga National Park (Day Trip from Guwahati)

**Distance:** 200 km from Guwahati, 4–5 hours drive. Best done as 2-night stay, not day trip.
**Highlight:** One-horned rhinoceros (world's largest population). Also tigers, elephants, water buffalo.
**Safari options:** Jeep safari (₹2,500–4,000 for 4 people) or elephant safari (₹1,500–2,000 per person, book in advance via Assam Tourism).
**Best season:** Nov–April. Park closed May–October (floods).

## Food in Guwahati and Northeast India

**Assamese cuisine characteristics:**
- Less spicy than North Indian standards, more sour (khar preparations).
- Rice-based; fish (rohu, hilsa) is the staple protein.
- Mustard oil used extensively.

**Vegetarian options:**
- Limited in local restaurants, but available.
- Dal, rice, sabzi available everywhere. South Indian restaurants (Udupi-style) present in Fancy Bazar area.
- Hotel restaurants in mid-range and premium hotels always have veg options.

**For families/kids:**
- Plain rice + dal available everywhere; request "no spice."
- Chinese food (noodles, fried rice) available and popular — mild flavors kids enjoy.
- Assam tea: famous; buy from Fancy Bazar shops. ₹150–400 per 250g pack.

**Notable restaurants:** Paradise (reliable veg + non-veg), Khorika (Assamese cuisine), Dynasty (Chinese/Thai, good for kids).

## General Northeast Notes

**Flight connectivity:** Guwahati (Lokpriya Gopinath Bordoloi International) has direct flights from Delhi, Kolkata, Mumbai, Bangalore, Chennai. Most Northeast destinations connect via Guwahati.

**Weather:**
- Oct–Feb: Cool and pleasant (10–20°C). Best season.
- Mar–May: Warm, some rain beginning April.
- Jun–Sep: Heavy monsoon. Some roads flooded. Kaziranga closed.

**Local transport in Guwahati:**
- Ola/Uber available.
- Pre-paid autos at railway station and airport; negotiate fares from hotels.
- City traffic: 8–10 AM and 5–8 PM peak; allow extra time.
