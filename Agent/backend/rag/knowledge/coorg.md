# Coorg Travel Knowledge

## Routing

**Standard routing: Bengaluru → Madikeri (Coorg HQ) → estate stays → Mysuru for departure**

Coorg has no railway station — all access is by road.
- Bengaluru → Madikeri: 4–5 hours (270 km via Mysuru or via Hassan). NH 275 to Mysuru then SH 88 is the smoother route.
- Mysuru → Madikeri: 2.5–3 hours (120 km). Mysuru is the better gateway if flying into the region.
- Mangaluru → Madikeri: 2.5 hours (135 km via Virajpet). Good exit option if continuing to Kerala.

Nearest railway station: Mysuru (for southern approach) or Kushalnagar (40 km from Madikeri; small station with limited trains from Bengaluru and Mysuru).

**Alternative routing: Bengaluru → Coorg → Wayanad → Kochi**
Popular combo — Coorg and Wayanad share a border; this loop works well for 7–10 day Kerala + Karnataka trips.

## Madikeri and Central Coorg

**Key places:** Raja's Seat, Madikeri Fort, Omkareshwara Temple, Abbey Falls (10 km from town)

**Raja's Seat:**
- Sunset viewpoint with manicured gardens. Entry: ₹10/adult.
- Toy train for kids inside garden: ₹20. Flat, accessible — suitable for all groups including elderly and strollers.
- Best at 5–6 PM for sunset; misty mornings also scenic.

**Abbey Falls:**
- Entry: ₹30. 15-minute walk from parking (uneven, slightly sloped path — manageable for most but not stroller-friendly).
- Best flow: Jul–Jan (post-monsoon). Reduced to a trickle by Apr–May.
- Avoid in peak monsoon (Jun–Jul) — path gets very slippery and water level dangerous.

**Madikeri Fort:** Free entry. Houses a museum and church. Flat grounds, suitable for all ages.

## Coffee Estate Stays and Experiences

**Coffee estate bungalows:**
- Typical price: ₹4,000–8,000/night for a full bungalow (2–3 bedrooms) on a working estate; meals often included.
- Estate walk with a planter guide is usually complimentary — 45–90 minutes, flat or gentle slopes through coffee and spice plants.
- Suitable for all ages; elderly can participate in the slow-paced walks. Kids enjoy the sensory experience (smelling cardamom, pepper vines, etc.).
- Reputable areas: Virajpet, Gonikoppal, Siddapur (more authentic, fewer resorts), Kushalnagar (more commercial, easier access).
- Trust issue: online listings for "estate stays" sometimes show generic resorts with a few coffee plants — check that the property is on an actual working estate. Ask for photos of the estate, not just the rooms.

**Dubare Elephant Camp:**
- Location: on the banks of River Cauvery, 35 km from Madikeri.
- Elephant interaction programme: ₹200–300/person. Feed, bathe, and walk with elephants.
- Timings: programme runs 9–11 AM daily. Arrive by 8:30 AM; entry closes once capacity is reached.
- Advance booking strongly recommended (Karnataka Forest Dept or via JLR — Jungle Lodges and Resorts): fills up fast on weekends and school holidays.
- Suitable for all ages including toddlers (carried by adults). Completely flat, river-access terrain.
- There is a JLR riverside camp at Dubare (₹8,000–12,000/night full board) if you want to stay close.

## Trekking and Wildlife

**Brahmagiri Wildlife Sanctuary:**
- 15 km from Virajpet. Sanctuary covers Nilgiris Biosphere Reserve.
- Entry: ₹150/person; guide mandatory (provided by forest department).
- Trekking trails range from 3 km to 18 km. The shorter circuit is manageable for families with kids 6+.
- NOT suitable for toddlers or elderly on longer trails — uneven jungle terrain.
- Best for birding (hornbills, Malabar trogon) and occasional sightings of gaur and elephants.

**Trekking in monsoon (Jun–Sep):** Heavy rain, leeches on all trails. If trekking in this season, wear full closed shoes (not sandals), tuck socks over trousers, apply salt/tobacco on shoes as deterrent. Do not wear open footwear at any estate during monsoon.

## General Coorg Notes

**Best time to visit:**
- Oct–Feb: Ideal. Clear skies, coffee harvest season, cool nights (12–18°C).
- Mar–May: Dry and warm (28–33°C). Less scenic (before monsoon), but easy travel.
- Jun–Sep: Heavy monsoon — Abbey Falls is dramatic, coffee estates lush green. But roads can flood, leech warnings for outdoors. Not recommended for trekking-focused trips. Estate stays are fine.
- Peak harvest: Oct–Dec — coffee and cardamom harvesting active; estate walks most experiential.

**Food:**
- Kodava (Coorgi) cuisine is pork-dominant: pandi curry (pork), kadambuttu (steamed rice balls), bamboo shoot curries, koli curry (chicken). Outstanding if you eat pork or chicken.
- Vegetarian options: significantly limited in local Kodava restaurants. Most estate stays and resorts provide vegetarian meals on request — confirm when booking.
- Kids: plain rice, roti, egg dishes available at most resorts; local restaurants may not cater well to plain food requests.
- Coorg honey and coffee are excellent to buy — standard prices at estate shops; inflated at tourist shops in Madikeri town.

**Transport:**
- No app-based cabs (Ola/Uber) within Coorg; rely on local taxis. Rates are fixed by local taxi unions.
- Madikeri taxi rates: local sightseeing full day ₹2,000–2,500. Bengaluru → Madikeri one-way ₹3,000–4,000.
- Renting bikes or scooters available in Madikeri town: ₹400–600/day — roads are good but ghat sections require care.

**Dietary and medical:** Carry personal medications; nearest multi-speciality hospital is in Mysuru (2.5 h away). Basic healthcare available at Madikeri District Hospital. Mobile network: BSNL/Airtel have coverage in main areas; Jio is patchy at remote estates — check before booking if connectivity matters.
