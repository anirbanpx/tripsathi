# Varanasi Travel Knowledge

## Routing

**Standard routing: Prayagraj → Varanasi**

Most travellers hit Varanasi as part of a UP circuit (Agra → Prayagraj → Varanasi). Prayagraj works as a natural buffer before Varanasi's density. The Delhi–Varanasi road distance makes train or flight almost always the better choice.

Driving times:
- Delhi → Varanasi: 12–14 hours via Yamuna Expressway then NH19 through Agra and Prayagraj — overnight trains (Kashi Express, Poorva Express) are significantly more practical for this distance
- Prayagraj → Varanasi: 2.5–3 hours via NH19 (120 km)
- Varanasi → Sarnath: 30–45 min (10 km; city traffic-dependent)
- Varanasi Airport → old city: 25–35 min (25 km via NH19)

**Alternative routing:** Fly into Lal Bahadur Shastri International Airport from Delhi (~1.5 hours, ₹3,500–8,000 one-way) — strongly recommended for elderly and families with toddlers. A 13-hour road journey through UP is genuinely punishing.

## The Ghats & Old City

**Key places:** Dashashwamedh Ghat, Manikarnika Ghat, Assi Ghat, Kashi Vishwanath Temple Corridor

**Dashashwamedh Ghat Aarti:**
- Entry: Free from ghat steps; boat view ₹200–500/person for 1-hour ride (negotiate before boarding)
- Evening aarti ~7 PM year-round (shifts with sunset; check locally). Ghat steps are steep and heavily crowded — toddlers must be held in arms throughout, not placed down. Elderly can sit on the upper platform benches or view from a boat; avoid the lower steps during aarti.
- Dev Deepawali (Kartik Purnima, Oct–Nov): ghats become impassable on foot; arrive 90 min early or pre-book a boat

**Kashi Vishwanath Temple:**
- Entry: Free for Indians; ₹300 foreigners. Photography prohibited inside — phones deposited at security.
- Post-2021 Corridor renovation: approach is now paved and wide; wheelchair-accessible entry available at the main gate. Queue: 1–3 hours general; ₹300 special darshan ticket reduces to 30 min. Factor 20 extra minutes for security with toddlers.
- Best time: 6–8 AM for a manageable queue; 3–4 AM Mangala Aarti for serious devotees

**Manikarnika Ghat:**
- Active 24-hour cremation ghat — photography prohibited and will cause confrontations. Not appropriate for young children or toddlers. Elderly: approach by boat only; old city lane is 60–80 cm wide in parts.

**Trust issue — boat ride price-switching:**
- Boatmen quote ₹100–200 for a "short ride." Once on the water they claim the fare was per ghat, demand ₹1,000–2,000 to return, or detour to a silk showroom mid-ride.
- Recommended approach: Fix price, duration, and return point before stepping into any boat. Use the pre-paid government boat counter at Dashashwamedh Ghat (₹200–250/person fixed rate); avoid freelance boatmen entirely.

**Ghats food:**
- Local dishes: Kachori Sabzi (fried kachori with aloo sabzi — the Banarasi breakfast staple, ₹30–50), Tamatar Chaat (spiced tomato base unique to Varanasi), Banarasi Lassi (thick, cream-topped in clay cups), Malaiyyo (seasonal frothy milk dessert, October–February only)
- For toddlers and young kids: Plain aloo sabzi from kachori stalls (without the fried shell) is soft and mild; banana and plain milk available at ghat stalls
- Vegetarian availability: Overwhelmingly vegetarian near ghats. Meat available in select lanes near Godowlia crossing.
- **Bhang warning:** Thandai and lassi are sometimes prepared with bhang (cannabis) without labelling. Say "plain, no bhang" explicitly when ordering at any ghat-side stall. Do not give either drink to children without confirming.

## Sarnath

**Key places:** Dhamek Stupa, Mulagandhakuti Vihara, Sarnath Archaeological Museum, Ashoka Pillar fragment

**Dhamek Stupa & ASI Compound:**
- Entry: ₹40/Indian adults, ₹600/foreigners; Sarnath Museum separate at ₹25/Indians, ₹300/foreigners
- Flat paved paths throughout — one of the most accessible major heritage sites in UP. Suitable for elderly and toddlers in a pram or carrier.
- Best time: 7–9 AM (sparse visitors, good light)
- Museum is fully air-conditioned — useful rest stop for elderly and toddlers in summer heat. Closed Fridays.

**Trust issue — unofficial "ASI guides" at Sarnath gate:**
- Men at the entry gate claim to be official ASI guides and quote ₹500+. Legitimate ASI guides are hired inside the gate at the official counter for ₹100–150.
- Recommended approach: Walk past touts at the gate, enter the compound, and hire from the ASI counter if a guide is needed. Site signage is adequate for most visitors.

**Sarnath food:**
- Restaurants near the site are tourist-priced (₹150–300/thali) and limited. Carry toddler snacks from Varanasi.
- Vegetarian availability: Entirely vegetarian around the Buddhist heritage zone.

## General Varanasi Notes

**Best time to visit:**
- October–March: Best overall. Ganges normalises post-monsoon, weather cool and dry (10–25°C). Dev Deepawali (November) is spectacular but requires 4–6 weeks advance hotel booking.
- April: Manageable. May–June brutal (42–46°C) — ghat visits viable only before 8 AM.
- July–September: Monsoon. Ganges floods lower ghats; boat rides restricted or cancelled in high flood years. Not recommended.

**Transport pricing:**
- Old city lanes near most ghats are too narrow for cars — final 200–500m must be walked regardless of transport mode.
- Auto-rickshaws: ₹60–120 within the tourist zone; negotiate upfront. No meters.
- Ola and Rapido operate for trips outside the old city lanes.
- Varanasi → Sarnath: ₹200–300 auto-rickshaw; ₹400–500 cab

**Dietary notes:**
- Predominantly vegetarian near ghat areas. Bhang is legal in Varanasi and sold in licensed shops — it enters the food supply informally in sweets, lassi, and thandai. Always confirm with vendors, especially for children.

**Medical:** Sir Sunderlal Hospital at BHU (Banaras Hindu University, ~5 km from ghats) is the best public facility in the city. Private option: Heritage Hospital near NH19. Keep children from all Ganges water contact — contamination levels in Varanasi are among the highest in the river's course year-round.
