# Manali Travel Knowledge

## Routing

**Standard routing: Chandigarh → Kullu → Manali**

This order provides staged altitude gain — Kullu at ~1,220m, Manali at ~2,050m — rather than going from plains to Manali in one overnight push. Critical for families with toddlers and elderly travellers who need 12–18 hours to acclimatise.

Driving times:
- Delhi → Manali: 13–15 hours via NH44 to Ambala, then NH21 (Chandigarh–Manali Highway) through Bilaspur and Mandi
- Chandigarh → Manali: 8–9 hours via NH21
- Manali Town → Solang Valley: 45 min (14 km on NH3 toward Rohtang)
- Manali Town → Rohtang Pass: 1.5–2 hours (51 km, NH3)

**Alternative routing:** Fly into Bhuntar Airport (Kullu, ~50 km south of Manali) and drive NH3 — saves 10+ road hours but flights cost ₹6,000–12,000 one-way from Delhi and are weather-cancellation-prone. Better for elderly travellers or families with toddlers avoiding a 14-hour bus journey.

## Manali Town & Old Manali

**Key places:** Mall Road, Hadimba Devi Temple, Vashisht hot springs, Old Manali village, Manu Temple

**Hadimba Devi Temple:**
- Entry: Free; ₹50 camera fee
- 200m of uneven stone paving to the entrance — pushchairs/prams cannot navigate it, use a baby carrier. Elderly need a companion for support. Steep stairs at the main shrine.
- Best time: 8–10 AM before tour groups arrive
- No seasonal closure; dress code enforced (covered shoulders and knees)

**Vashisht Hot Springs:**
- Entry: ₹30–50/person (public baths, separate male/female)
- Water exceeds 40°C — toddlers must not enter. Elderly with cardiac conditions or hypertension should skip.
- Best time: Early morning; cleaner and less crowded

**Trust issue — photo touts at Hadimba Temple:**
- Men with "traditional" Himachali caps, yaks, and horses position themselves on the approach path. Once they place a cap on a child's head or pose a family, they demand ₹300–500 per photo.
- Recommended approach: Decline before any physical contact. Walk past without eye contact. A cap placed on a child is considered a completed transaction.

**Manali Town food:**
- Local dishes: Siddu (wheat bread stuffed with walnut paste or poppy seeds, served with ghee and dal), Rajma Chawal (Himachali kidney bean curry over rice), Babru (black gram-stuffed fried bread)
- For toddlers and young kids: Plain boiled rice with dal available at most dhabas on request; Siddu without filling can be ordered at Old Manali cafés
- Vegetarian availability: Strong — Old Manali and Mall Road are predominantly vegetarian. Tibetan momos and thukpa contain meat frequently; confirm before ordering.

## Solang Valley & Rohtang Corridor

**Key places:** Solang Valley snow zone, Rohtang Pass (3,978m), Atal Tunnel North Portal / Sissu (3,100m)

**Solang Valley:**
- Entry: Free; activities extra — snowmobile ₹300–500, zorbing ₹300, skiing lessons ₹500–800/hour
- Toddlers can play in base snow safely; use a baby carrier, not a pram. Elderly can reach the main zone via a 200m flat walk from parking.
- Best time: 9 AM–noon; queues and crowds peak from noon
- Snow activities: November–March; paragliding and ATV: July–September

**Rohtang Pass:**
- Permit: ₹550/vehicle (non-Himachal registered) at rohtangpermits.nic.in, book 1–2 days ahead; daily cap of 1,200 diesel + 800 petrol vehicles
- Not suitable for toddlers under 2 or elderly with respiratory/cardiac conditions — altitude is 3,978m, no medical facility within 30 km
- Open May–June and September–October only. Closed July–August (landslides on NH3) and November–April.
- Depart Manali by 7 AM — congestion builds by 10 AM, permit entry window closes at 1 PM

**Trust issue — fake Rohtang permit agents:**
- Touts near Manali hotels offer to "arrange" permits for ₹1,500–2,000, claiming the portal is full or too complex. The portal is straightforward and cancellations free up slots daily.
- Recommended approach: Book rohtangpermits.nic.in the evening before. If sold out, drive through Atal Tunnel to Sissu — no permit needed, reaches 3,100m with sufficient snow and river views.

**Solang/Rohtang food:**
- Roadside stalls only: Maggi, momos, tea, corn-on-the-cob (₹30–60/item)
- For toddlers: Carry food from Manali town — no allergen control at mountain stalls
- Vegetarian availability: Stall food is mostly vegetarian by default

## General Manali Notes

**Best time to visit:**
- March–June: Best overall. Snow at Rohtang and Solang, all roads open by mid-April. May–June is peak: hotel rates 2–3× higher; book 3–4 weeks ahead.
- July–August: Monsoon. Rohtang closed, NH21 landslide delays of 4–8 hours common. Not recommended for families or elderly. Solo backpackers get 40–50% lower hotel rates.
- September–October: Second-best window. Rohtang reopens September, crowds thin, weather clear. Best for couples and senior travellers.
- November–February: Manali town accessible but Solang and Rohtang snowbound; road conditions unpredictable. Not suitable for toddlers or mobility-limited travellers.

**Transport pricing:**
- No Ola or Uber — taxi union controls all cabs. Negotiate fare before boarding.
- Manali Town → Solang Valley round trip: ₹700–900 private; ₹100–150/seat shared (from HRTC bus stand)
- Rohtang Pass full-day taxi: ₹2,500–3,500

**Dietary notes:**
- Dominant fats are ghee and mustard oil. Tibetan momos at street stalls often contain pork — confirm before ordering.
- Vegetarian options widely available at all price points.

**Medical:** Lady Willingdon Hospital (Government Regional Hospital, Manali) handles routine emergencies and mild altitude sickness. Serious cases require evacuation to IGMC Shimla (250 km) or AIIMS Delhi. Carry Diamox (acetazolamide) for Rohtang and above — consult a doctor before use with children or elderly.
