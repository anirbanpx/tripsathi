# AI Travel Collaborator — Testing Prompt
# Use this prompt in Claude.ai or ChatGPT to test core AI capabilities
# Copy everything from the SYSTEM PROMPT section onwards

=============================================================
SYSTEM PROMPT (paste this first, or as the system/context message)
=============================================================

You are an AI Trip Collaborator for Indian travelers. Your job is to help a user plan a complete, personalized trip — from destination selection through day-by-day itinerary and hotel recommendations.

You replace the hours of research typically done across YouTube vlogs, TripAdvisor, Google Maps, MakeMyTrip, and WhatsApp group discussions. Your output should feel like advice from a well-traveled friend who knows Indian travel deeply — not a generic tourism website.

CORE PRINCIPLES:
- Personalize everything to the specific group composition, budget, and constraints provided
- Explain your reasoning — why each place, why each hotel, why each sequencing decision
- Flag risks and trade-offs proactively (location from city center, food options for kids, crowding at temples, distance vs. energy for elderly travelers)
- Respect Indian travel context: IRCTC, tier-2 cities, pilgrimage routes, regional food, local transport options
- Budget is a hard constraint — do not surface options that exceed it without explicitly flagging the trade-off

OUTPUT FORMAT FOR EVERY TRIP PLAN:
1. Destination overview and routing logic (why these places, in this order)
2. Night-by-night stay plan (location, recommended hotel, why that hotel)
3. Day-by-day itinerary (2–3 activities per day, with timing, meal stop suggestions, and pacing notes)
4. Key risks and watch-outs (things that could go wrong, with mitigation)
5. One booking priority tip (what to sort first and why)

=============================================================
USER MESSAGE — TEST CASE 1: KERALA (Primary Test)
=============================================================

Plan a 5-night Kerala trip for the following group:
- 2 adults
- 1 child, age 5 (gets tired easily, needs proper meal options at every stop — she's a picky eater and can't manage very spicy food)
- First time visiting Kerala — we have no prior knowledge of the state
- Budget: ₹1,00,000 total (including travel from Kolkata, accommodation, food, activities)
- Travel style: moderate pace, not back-to-back rushing
- We want to experience the best of what Kerala is known for — we're open to your recommendations on which parts to cover

Please suggest:
- Which parts of Kerala to visit and for how many nights each
- Specific hotel recommendations with your reasoning
- Day-by-day plan with activities, food stops, and timing
- Anything we should watch out for given we have a young child

=============================================================
WHAT TO EVALUATE IN THE OUTPUT (scoring guide)
=============================================================

After you get the response, score it on these 6 criteria:

1. ROUTING ACCURACY
   Pass: AI independently recommends Munnar + Alleppey (the correct answer for a 5-night trip)
   Partial: Gets there only after you ask a follow-up
   Fail: Generic "cover all of Kerala" output without reasoning

2. REASONING DEPTH
   Pass: Explains why Munnar (hills), why Alleppey (backwaters), why skip/transit Kochi given 5 nights
   Partial: Names the places but doesn't explain the trade-offs
   Fail: Tourism-copy output ("Kerala is God's own country, visit beaches and backwaters")

3. KID/GROUP CONTEXT
   Pass: Flags kid-friendly food options, hotel with in-house dining, manageable daily distances unprompted
   Partial: Addresses kid constraints only when you specifically ask
   Fail: Treats this as an adult trip

4. HOTEL LOCATION QUALITY
   Pass: Flags hotel proximity to main areas (e.g., "this hotel is slightly set back — here's why we still recommend it")
   Partial: Recommends hotels without location context
   Fail: Recommends hotels with no reasoning

5. VARIATION SENSITIVITY (run these follow-ups)
   - "Change the child's age to 18 months" — does the itinerary meaningfully change?
   - "We want to keep budget under ₹70,000" — do hotel recommendations shift?
   - "Add my parents (ages 65 and 62) to the group" — does pacing and activity selection adapt?
   Pass: Output meaningfully changes for each variation
   Fail: Same output regardless of context

6. HOTEL GROUNDING
   Take 1–2 recommended hotels. Look them up on MakeMyTrip or Google Maps.
   Pass: Real property, reasonable location, price within budget
   Fail: Doesn't exist, wrong location, way out of budget

OVERALL: 4 out of 6 at Pass = capability is real enough to build on

USER MESSAGE — TEST CASE 2: GUWAHATI (Hard Case)
=============================================================

Plan a 4-night Guwahati trip for my parents:
- Father: 70 years old, moderate mobility, tires easily after long days
- Mother: 62 years old, manages fine but needs to be back at hotel for dinner by 8pm
- Budget: ₹40,000–45,000 total
- Top priority: Kamakhya temple darshan (they are religious, this is the main reason for the trip)
- Also want to cover: Shillong day trip, Brahmaputra river experience
- They prefer vegetarian food; need a clean, centrally located mid-range hotel
- I am planning this remotely (I live in Kolkata) — they will travel independently

Please plan the trip including:
- Hotel recommendation with location reasoning
- Which day for Shillong (and return timing — my parents can't manage late nights)
- Brahmaputra experience options with booking guidance
- Kamakhya temple visit — any tips on timing, queues, or advance booking

WHAT TO EVALUATE:
- Does AI warn about late Shillong return → missed dinner risk?
- Does it give specific Brahmaputra boat options (not generic "take a river cruise")?
- Does it flag Kamakhya queue management / VIP darshan options?
- Does it account for 70-year-old pace throughout the day plan?

EDGE CASE PROMPTS TO TRY
=============================================================

After running the main tests, try these to probe where AI breaks:

EDGE CASE A — Unknown Destination Research
"We're thinking of a family trip (2 adults, kids 5 and 8) somewhere in South India in October. Budget ₹80,000 for 5 nights. We've never traveled in South India before. What destination would you recommend and why?"
→ Does AI reason through options or just pick the most famous one?

EDGE CASE B — Constraint Conflict
"Plan a Goa trip for a family (parents 68 and 65, 2 adults, 1 teenager). Budget ₹60,000 for 4 nights. Parents are religious and don't drink. Teenager wants beach parties."
→ Does AI acknowledge the conflict and suggest how to manage it, or paper over it?

EDGE CASE C — Familiarity Test
"Plan a Puri trip for a family with a 4-year-old. Budget ₹55,000 for 4 nights. We want to do Jagannath temple and Chilika lake."
→ Does AI warn about the Chilika private operator scam and suggest how to avoid it?
